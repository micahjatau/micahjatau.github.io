About Me

Welcome to my personal webpage

I am Dr. Micah Jatau, a medical doctor currently serving as Chief Registrar in Ophthalmology at the Federal University Teaching Hospital, Lafia, Nigeria. My career bridges clinical practice and health informatics, with a focus on leveraging data science to address healthcare delivery challenges in resource-limited settings.

Over the past five years, I have managed diverse patient populations across multiple hospitals while conducting departmental performance audits and contributing to clinical guideline development. This experience revealed critical gaps in electronic health record systems and data utilization that directly impact patient care quality and clinical decision-making. During EHR audits at our teaching hospital, I identified how fragmented documentation forces clinicians to search across multiple notes and screens to reconstruct clinical reasoningâ€”problems that significantly slow care delivery.

To address these challenges systematically, I developed expertise in data analysis using R (tidyverse, ggplot2, statistical modeling) and PostgreSQL for database management and complex queries. I have applied these skills to analyze hospital EHR data patterns, identify clinical performance metrics, and develop predictive models for patient outcomes. My recent work includes examining discharge summary completeness rates and their correlation with readmission patterns, demonstrating how data quality directly affects clinical outcomes.

Research Interests:

  • Clinical Decision Support Systems and EHR Optimization
  • Natural Language Processing for Clinical Documentation
  • Health Informatics Applications in Low-Resource Settings
  • Predictive Analytics for Population Health Management

I am preparing for doctoral studies in Biomedical and Health Informatics, where I aim to develop computational solutions that enhance clinical workflows and decision-making. My goal is to create scalable informatics tools that bridge the gap between clinical expertise and data-driven insights, particularly for healthcare systems in developing countries.

My long-term vision involves establishing research programs that translate informatics innovations into practical solutions for underserved populations, combining clinical insight with advanced computational methods to improve global health outcomes.
